What is Carbon FootPrint?

A carbon footprint is defined as the total amount of greenhouse gases emitted into the atmosphere, such as carbon dioxide (CO2), methane (CH4), nitrous oxide (N2O), hydrofluorocarbons (HFCs), expressed in equivalent tons of CO2. It may be associated with the activities of an individual, a community, or even a product.

Causes of Carbon FootPrint

FOSSIL FUELS

  • The combustion of fossil fuels is a primary contributor to carbon emissions and other greenhouse gases.

  • From the manufacturing of goods to their transportation using freight trains, many aspects of our daily activities involve the consumption of fossil fuels, further intensifying carbon footprints.

MODERN LIFESTYLE

  • In our contemporary society, the pursuit of an image often revolves around owning the latest electronic equipment.
  • The production and use of these gadgets, from mobile processors to power-driven home appliances, contribute substantially to the carbon dioxide in the atmosphere.
  • Additionally, the prevalence of cars, with a theoretical count exceeding seven billion if each person had one, significantly amplifies carbon emissions.

FOOD

  • The carbon footprint associated with food consumption is noteworthy, particularly when relying on processed or non-locally produced items.
  • The production and transportation of food contribute to greenhouse gas emissions, adding to the overall environmental impact of our dietary choices.

AIR TRAVEL

  • Despite the affordability and convenience of air travel, it comes at an environmental cost. The carbon dioxide emissions from aircraft, approximately 90 kg per hour, contribute significantly to the overall carbon footprint.
  • The proliferation of airports in various cities further compounds the environmental impact of air travel.
